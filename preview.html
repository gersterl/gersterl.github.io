rArm —
Print-Oriented Robotic Arm

rArm is my current robotic arm project. The concept is a belt-driven system using NEMA 17 stepper motors and approximately 1:19.2 gear reductions, with a strong focus on making as much of the mechanical system as possible on the 3D printer.

The central technical highlight is the development of my own compact cycloidal drives. I use the project to combine CAD iteration, drivetrain packaging, 3D-printable geometry, electronics integration, and testing under real assembly constraints.

  • Designed around printable parts, modular joints, and compact actuator packaging.
  • Custom cycloidal-drive development for reduced backlash and high reduction in limited space.
  • Belt-driven architecture using NEMA 17 motors and geared output stages.
  • Built as a demonstrator of persistence, design iteration, and focus-project readiness.

Manufacturing

Prototype Fabrication

I design with manufacturing constraints in mind, especially for 3D printing. The rArm project is built around producing as many components as possible on a Bambu X1E, which forces careful thinking about tolerances, print orientation, stiffness, assembly, and repeatability.

I have also worked with CO₂ laser cutting, giving me additional experience with fast physical iteration and flat-stock prototyping.
